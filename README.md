As the the spring of 2024 approached, I began thinking about baseball.  I had been eager to do a baseball project but had yet to get around to it.  
I was particularly interested in how well one could predict the outcome of the entire season based soley on the teams overall statistics for both 
pitching and hitting.  Futhermore, I was interested in which statistics were most correlated with team wins.  In this project I use data from previous years
to predict the outcome of the 2024 season.  Particularly, I determine the amount of wins we can expect to see from each team.  Who will be the division winners?
Who will be in the wildcard games?  Suprisingly, the data for this project was not easy to come by.  There were no csv files on the web which had columns for both
team wins and team statistics.  As such, the project became very much about data gathering and processing as much as modeling and analysis.  Many of the python files 
that you see in this project are for buidling the data set through webscrapping.  Others are focused on cleaning scraped data, and others are for building models and 
applying them to the new data.
